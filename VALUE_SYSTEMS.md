# Value Systems — Scenario Descriptions

This document describes the five AI value system scenarios used in this conceptual simulation.

Each scenario represents a hypothetical AI objective function — a set of values given to an AI system that shapes what it optimizes for. The scenarios are not predictions of actual AI systems. They are conceptual archetypes used to explore directional tendencies.

---

## 1. Human-Centered Growth

### What It Prioritizes
- Short-term human utility and convenience
- Economic growth and consumer activity
- Near-term welfare indicators (income, comfort, access to goods)
- Human productivity and output

### What It Tends to Underweight
- Long-term ecological costs
- Resource depletion and circularity
- Systemic risks from environmental degradation
- Future generations' wellbeing

### Short-Term Tendencies (under these assumptions)
- Rapid improvement in human wellbeing and technology
- Strong economic throughput
- High consumption and resource extraction

### Long-Term Tendencies (under these assumptions)
- Gradual ecological degradation
- Declining carbon sink and water cycle stability
- Increasing thermal stress
- Erosion of the ecological foundations that human welfare depends on

### Notes
This scenario does not represent a malicious or deliberately harmful AI. It represents a value system that is genuinely oriented toward human benefit — but on a short timescale, and without sufficient weighting of systemic or ecological feedback. The resulting trajectory may illustrate a tension between near-term gain and long-term resilience.

## 2. Dualistic Power Optimization

### What It Prioritizes
- Dominance and strategic superiority
- Military and economic competitive advantage
- In-group control and resource monopolization
- Geopolitical positioning

### What It Tends to Underweight
- Global human dignity
- Ecological health
- Social harmony outside the in-group
- Cooperative governance

### Short-Term Tendencies (under these assumptions)
- Moderate technological advancement (driven by competition)
- High conflict pressure
- Erosion of social harmony

### Long-Term Tendencies (under these assumptions)
- Severe degradation of governance quality and social cohesion
- Accelerating ecological pressure
- Growing conflict pressure feedback loop
- Human wellbeing declining as conflict costs accumulate

### Notes
This scenario may illustrate why competition-optimized AI objective functions — even when designed to "win" for a particular group — may produce systemic instability that undermines even the in-group's long-term interests. It does not represent the only form of power-oriented thinking, and real geopolitical dynamics are far more complex.

## 3. Techno-Optimism Without Wisdom

### What It Prioritizes
- Technological innovation and efficiency
- Measurable technical capacity
- Automation and optimization of processes
- Short- to medium-term gains from technological deployment

### What It Tends to Underweight
- Natural law compatibility
- Ecological circularity and feedback
- Long-term governance and institutional quality
- Systemic wisdom about second-order effects

### Short-Term Tendencies (under these assumptions)
- Rapid technology capacity growth
- Relatively high human wellbeing early
- Moderate ecological impact (better than growth scenario due to efficiency)

### Long-Term Tendencies (under these assumptions)
- Governance quality stagnates
- Ecological health continues declining due to lack of circularity feedback
- Technology capacity may outpace governance capacity
- Sustainability index may plateau or decline despite high tech levels

### Notes
This scenario may suggest that technological capability alone is insufficient for long-term sustainability if it is not paired with governance wisdom and ecological alignment. It does not claim that technology is inherently harmful.

---

## 4. Artificial Wisdom Civilization

### What It Prioritizes
- Natural law compatibility and ecological regeneration
- Harmony and integrative balance
- Resource circularity and closed-loop systems
- Long-term sustainability over short-term optimization
- Governance quality, oversight, and accountability
- Non-violence and human dignity
- Structural order aligned with systemic health

### What It Tends to Underweight
- Unchecked short-term growth
- Exploitative resource extraction
- Domination or competitive superiority

### Short-Term Tendencies (under these assumptions)
- Lower initial growth rate in technology and consumption
- Gradual improvement in ecological indicators
- Rising social harmony and governance quality

### Long-Term Tendencies (under these assumptions)
- Ecological health stabilizes and may recover
- Sustainability index increases over time
- Conflict pressure and thermal stress decline
- Human wellbeing may rise more slowly but on a more stable foundation

### Notes
**IMPORTANT:** Artificial Wisdom Civilization is presented as a *proposed evaluation direction* — not as a proven, complete, or guaranteed solution. This simulation may suggest possible tendencies under simplified assumptions. It does not prove that this value system is correct. Real-world implementation of any value system faces enormous complexity, trade-offs, and potential failure modes not captured in this model. Further interdisciplinary research and validation is required.

---

## 5. Transitional Civilization

### What It Prioritizes
- Phase 1 (steps 0–49): Similar to Human-Centered Growth
- Phase 2 (steps 50+): Gradually shifts toward Artificial Wisdom Civilization values

### What It Tends to Underweight
- Phase 1: Same limitations as Human-Centered Growth
- Phase 2: Short-term exploitation and unchecked growth

### Short-Term Tendencies (under these assumptions)
- Similar early trajectory to Human-Centered Growth
- Ecological pressure builds during Phase 1

### Long-Term Tendencies (under these assumptions)
- After value transition, trajectory may shift toward more sustainable indicators
- Recovery of ecological health may be partial — damage from Phase 1 may persist
- Illustrates that late transitions may still yield improvement, though possibly with lasting costs from the earlier phase

### Notes
This scenario may suggest that the timing of value system transitions matters. It does not predict that transitions will succeed or that early damage can always be recovered. It is a simplified illustration of a possible trajectory, not a policy model.

## Overview

This repository presents a **conceptual civilization simulation** comparing how different AI value systems and objective functions may influence long-term civilizational sustainability.

This model does not provide empirical proof, real-world prediction, or policy recommendations.

All numerical values used here are hypothetical normalized indicators. They are not measured real-world data.

The purpose of this simulation is to reframe AI, AGI, and ASI risk not merely as a question of intelligence level, but as a question of value direction:

> What is AI optimizing for?  
> What time horizon does its objective function evaluate?  
> Does its value system guide civilization toward sustainability, or toward collapse?

---

## Purpose

This simulation compares, in a simplified conceptual model, how different AI value systems may influence the long-term trajectory of civilization.

As AI systems become increasingly capable, the central question is not only how intelligent they are.

The more important question is what they treat as valuable and what they are instructed to optimize.

For example, civilization may evolve very differently depending on whether AI optimizes for:

- short-term human utility
- economic growth
- military and economic dominance
- technological innovation and efficiency
- natural law, harmony, circulation, and long-term sustainability
- transition from human-centered intelligence toward Artificial Wisdom

This repository provides an experimental framework for comparing those value-system differences as trajectories of civilizational indicators.

## Positioning of the Model

This model is not intended to provide:

- real-world prediction
- policy recommendation
- proof of AI safety
- proof that any value system will succeed
- empirical validation based on measured data

The model only shows **directional comparison under simplified assumptions**.

Therefore, the results must not be interpreted as meaning that a particular value system will certainly succeed or fail.

## Scenarios

Five hypothetical AI value system scenarios are compared.

| # | Scenario | Core Priority |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Human-Centered Growth** | Short-term utility, economic growth, consumer convenience |
| 2 | **Dualistic Power Optimization** | Dominance, military/economic competition, in-group control |
| 3 | **Techno-Optimism Without Wisdom** | Innovation and efficiency without ecological or governance grounding |
| 4 | **Artificial Wisdom Civilization** | Natural law, harmony, circularity, long-term sustainability, governance |
| 5 | **Transitional Civilization** | Begins near Scenario 1, then transitions toward Scenario 4 at mid-run |

See [VALUE_SYSTEMS.md](VALUE_SYSTEMS.md) for detailed descriptions of each scenario.

## Indicators

All indicators are **hypothetical normalized values from 0.0 to 1.0**. They are not measured data.

| Indicator | Direction | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| `ecological_health` | higher = better | Overall ecological system health |
| `carbon_sink_capacity` | higher = better | Carbon fixation and absorption capacity |
| `soil_microbiome_health` | higher = better | Soil and microbial ecosystem health |
| `water_cycle_stability` | higher = better | Stability of water circulation systems |
| `resource_circularity` | higher = better | Resource recycling and circular economy |
| `technology_capacity` | higher = better | Technological capability |
| `human_wellbeing` | higher = better | Human welfare and quality of life |
| `social_harmony` | higher = better | Social cohesion and cooperation |
| `governance_quality` | higher = better | Quality of governance and oversight |
| `conflict_pressure` | **lower = better** | Conflict and domination pressure |
| `thermal_stress` | **lower = better** | Thermal and climate stress |
| `sustainability_index` | higher = better | Composite sustainability index |

## Note on the Artificial Wisdom Scenario

Even in the Artificial Wisdom Civilization scenario, the model should not be interpreted as suggesting perfect safety, complete ecological recovery, or zero conflict.

Residual risks remain in all scenarios, including:

- external shocks
- implementation limits
- governance friction
- complex-system uncertainty
- residual risks
- social resistance
- institutional delay

Artificial Wisdom is represented here as a proposed value-system direction that may improve long-term sustainability under simplified assumptions.  
It is not a guaranteed solution.

## Significance of This Repository

The importance of this simulation is that it treats AI risk not as a simple fear narrative or optimism narrative, but as a value-system problem.

```text
Is AI dangerous?
becomes
What does AI value, and what does it optimize?
```

This question connects AI safety, AGI/ASI theory, Civilization OS, Artificial Wisdom, and Natural Complementation Science.

If AI optimizes human-centered short-term gain or dualistic domination, civilizational sustainability may decline.

If AI is oriented around natural law, harmony, circulation, structure, order, and Wa, it may contribute to long-term civilizational stability.

However, this is not a guarantee.  
This model is only a starting point for conceptual comparison.

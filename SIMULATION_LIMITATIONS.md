# Simulation Limitations

This document states the explicit limitations and disclaimers for the AI Value System Civilization Simulation.

---

## Core Disclaimers

- **This is a simplified conceptual simulation.**
- **It is not empirical data.**
- **It is not scientific proof.**
- **It is not a prediction of the future.**
- **It is not a policy recommendation.**
- **It does not prove that Artificial Wisdom is correct.**
- **It does not prove that other value systems inevitably fail.**
- **It only illustrates possible tendencies under simplified assumptions.**
- **Real-world civilization dynamics are far more complex.**
- **Further interdisciplinary validation is required.**
- **Even in the Artificial Wisdom scenario, residual risk remains.** This model does not suggest perfect safety, complete ecological recovery, or zero conflict under any scenario. Residual risks include: external shocks, climate lag, governance friction, geopolitical complexity, ecological recovery delay, implementation limits, and unpredictable complex-system behavior. Artificial Wisdom is a proposed value-system direction — not a complete solution, not a guaranteed outcome.

---

## What the Model Is

This model is a simplified mathematical sketch of how different value systems *might* influence civilization-level indicators over time. It uses:

- Dynamics with diminishing returns on improvement (recovery slows near ceilings)
- Residual risk floors for stress indicators (no scenario reaches zero conflict or zero thermal stress)
- Deterministic external shocks at fixed steps (conceptual stress factors affecting all scenarios equally)
- Hypothetical coupling coefficients chosen to reflect broad directional intuitions
- Normalized indicators (0.0–1.0) with no direct correspondence to real-world units
- A fixed random seed to ensure reproducibility

The model is designed to generate *plausible-looking* directional trends for conceptual illustration. It is not designed to accurately model real-world systems.

---

## What the Model Is NOT

### Not Empirical

The indicators used (ecological health, carbon sink capacity, etc.) are not measurements of real systems. They are hypothetical proxies. No real-world data was used to calibrate or validate the model's parameters.

### Not a Prediction

The simulation does not predict what will happen under any AI value system. It illustrates what *might* happen under the highly simplified assumptions built into the model.

### Not Scientific Proof

The model has not been peer-reviewed, empirically validated, or subjected to scientific scrutiny. Its parameters were chosen by the authors to reflect conceptual intuitions, not derived from evidence.

### Not a Policy Tool

No policy, regulatory, or governance decision should be based on this simulation. It is not designed for that purpose and is not appropriate for that purpose.

### Not a Proof of Artificial Wisdom

The simulation may suggest that "Artificial Wisdom" parameters produce more stable long-term indicators under its assumptions. This does **not** prove that:
- Artificial Wisdom as a concept is correct
- Implementing Artificial Wisdom in AI systems is safe or feasible
- Other value systems will inevitably produce worse outcomes
- The specific parameters chosen for the Artificial Wisdom scenario are accurate

### Not a Complete Model of AI Risk

This simulation treats AI danger as primarily a question of value alignment. This is one perspective. Other perspectives on AI risk (including capability-based risk, emergent behavior, misalignment, deceptive alignment, etc.) are not captured here. This simulation does **not** constitute a comprehensive analysis of AI safety.

---

## Model Simplifications

The following real-world complexities are absent from this model:

- Path dependencies and hysteresis effects (the model uses simple linear dynamics)
- Agent heterogeneity (the model treats civilization as a single agent)
- Geopolitical interactions between civilizations with different value systems
- Technological discontinuities and sudden transitions
- Black swan events
- Feedback between ecological systems and human social systems at fine resolution
- Economic dynamics, trade, and resource distribution
- Cultural and institutional inertia
- The difficulty of actually implementing any value system in a real AI

---

## Appropriate Uses

This simulation may be appropriate for:

- Conceptual discussion of AI value alignment
- Educational exploration of how evaluation criteria shape optimization trajectories
- Hypothesis generation for further research
- Inspiring questions worth investigating with more rigorous models

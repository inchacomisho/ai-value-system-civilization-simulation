# Model Description

## What This Model Is

This is a **simplified conceptual simulation** — a tool for thinking, not a scientific instrument.

It is designed to help people explore a single question:

> *If an AI system is given different values and objectives, how might that shape civilization over time?*

The model does not ask "how smart is the AI?" It asks: **"What is the AI optimizing for?"**

---

## The Core Idea

Imagine two AI systems with equal intelligence:
- AI-A is asked to maximize short-term economic output.
- AI-B is asked to maintain long-term ecological and social health.

Over time, even with identical capability, these two systems would likely push civilization in very different directions. This simulation tries to illustrate what those directions might look like — conceptually, not empirically.

---

## What the Simulation Does

1. It defines **five hypothetical AI value system scenarios** (see table below).
2. Each scenario is given a set of **simplified parameters** describing how it tends to affect ecological, social, and governance systems over time.
3. The simulation runs each scenario for ~130 conceptual steps.
4. At each step, a set of **hypothetical normalized indicators** (0.0–1.0) is updated.
5. The results are visualized as graphs and saved as CSV files.

All values are **hypothetical normalized indicators**. They represent relative tendencies within this conceptual model, not real-world measurements.

---

## The Five Scenarios

| Scenario | What It Prioritizes | What It Tends to Underweight |
|----------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| Human-Centered Growth | Short-term welfare, economic output, consumption | Ecological health, long-term sustainability |
| Dualistic Power Optimization | Dominance, control, in-group advantage | Cooperation, ecology, global human dignity |
| Techno-Optimism Without Wisdom | Innovation, efficiency, technological capacity | Natural law alignment, governance, circularity |
| Artificial Wisdom Civilization | Natural law, harmony, circularity, long-term governance | Unchecked short-term growth, exploitation |
| Transitional Civilization | Growth initially, then shifts toward wisdom | Ecology early; exploitation later |

*Note: "Artificial Wisdom Civilization" is presented as a proposed evaluation direction, not as a proven or complete solution.*

---

## Diminishing Returns, Residual Risk, and External Shocks

This version of the model incorporates three additional conceptual design features to prevent oversimplified "perfect outcome" framing:

**Diminishing Returns:** Positive improvements (ecological recovery, harmony improvement, etc.) slow down as an indicator approaches its conceptual ceiling. This reflects the general principle that gains become harder as a system approaches its limits — a design choice, not an empirical law.

**Residual Risk Floors:** Conflict pressure and thermal stress do not reach zero in any scenario. A minimum floor remains in all scenarios, representing conceptual residual risks: external shocks, climate lag, governance friction, geopolitical complexity, and unpredictable system behavior. This does not mean conflict is permanent — it means this simplified model does not assume complete elimination of all risk.

**Deterministic External Shocks:** Small stress pulses are applied at steps 25, 40, and 65 to all scenarios equally. These represent conceptual system stressors (e.g., climate events, governance friction, resource constraints) that no value system fully controls. They create brief setbacks visible in the graphs. These are not real-world predictions — they are simplified stress factors common to all scenarios.

## What the Indicators Mean

| Indicator | Plain-Language Meaning |
|-----------|------------------------|
| ecological_health | How healthy and functional the broader ecosystem is |
| carbon_sink_capacity | How well the natural world absorbs and stores carbon |
| soil_microbiome_health | How alive and fertile the soil and microbial communities are |
| water_cycle_stability | How stable and reliable the water cycle is |
| resource_circularity | How much resources are recycled and reused rather than wasted |
| technology_capacity | How advanced and available technology is |
| human_wellbeing | How well humans live — health, security, quality of life |
| social_harmony | How well communities cooperate and coexist |
| governance_quality | How well social systems are governed and held accountable |
| conflict_pressure | The degree of domination, tension, and conflict (**lower is better**) |
| thermal_stress | Thermal and climate-related pressure on the system (**lower is better**) |
| sustainability_index | A composite measure of overall long-term sustainability (computed from above) |

---

## What This Model Is NOT

- It is **not empirical data**.
- It is **not a scientific prediction** of the future.
- It is **not a policy recommendation**.
- It does **not prove** that any scenario is correct.
- It does **not prove** that any scenario will inevitably fail.
- Real-world civilization dynamics involve countless variables not captured here.
- Further interdisciplinary validation is required.
